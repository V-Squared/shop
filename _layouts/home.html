Breakthrough: Modular-in-One Form Factor

Although the Modular-in-One looks like an All-in-One it does not inherit the many drawbacks of the All-in-One form factor. E.g. it is as fast as a desktop and almost as cost efficient. Instead it merges the best of Desktop, Mini-PC and All-in-One. This is quite a feat — offering lots of benefits for you — and may sound too good to be true. This is why we give you a deeper look of just how much better the ViDock-One is, the first implementation of the Modular-in-One form factor.

Display Freedom of Modular-in-One
While All-in-One locks you in, the Modular-in-One sets you free to choose the right display for your needs and budget:

  • Freedom
  • Independent
  • Upgrading
  • How it Works

The display is the single most important interface to your computer. That's why they come with a staggering amount of sizes and features. Power users know that picking the right combination of size, resolution and features for your application will make a huge difference. In turn, leaving features you do not need behind will save you hundreds of dollars. Desktop users have enjoyed this modularity since 1981. For All-in-One users this modularity is introduced for the first time with the Modular-in-One form factor.

When buying an All-in-One the better displays are tied to better CPU, better GPU and bigger disks. With Modular-in-One we empower you to choose every component independently and tailored to your needs. e.g. you may pair a big display with a small CPU if all you want to do is watching movies.

Say you own a Mini-PC and a display. Thanks to the modular nature of Modular-in-One you can keep your computer parts and upgrade them to an All-in-One form factor. If you already own a Modular-in-One, then you can upgrade the display or the computer independently, while keeping the remaining parts. This extends the life span of your computer parts, thus saving you money and it saves our planet's resources while avoiding toxic computer trash.

Obviously modularity is the key. ViDock-One supports the industry standard VESA mount with both sizes 75mm and 100mm, which are the used sizes in the supported display size range of 17" … 34". ViDock-One supports heavy displays of up to 15kg. The VESA-mounting plate is small, to be compatible to a 100mm recessed VESA mount, as it is common with some display vendors, such as Dell.
